## 1. Breathe

* **Type:** Automatic, always active
* **Description:** A constant area of calm that gently damages and slows nearby stressors, pulsing with the rhythm of breathing.
* **Activation:** Always active
* **Base Stats (Agnostic):**
  * Damage: 5 DPS (base), scales with upgrades
  * Slow: 50% slow (0.5x speed multiplier) applied while in AoE
  * AoE Radius: Pulses between 100-140px (player radius + buffer + growth)
  * Cycle Duration: 8 seconds (inhale + exhale)
  * Scaling: Linear (+10% per level damage, +5% per level radius)
* **Mechanics:**
  * **Offensive:** Applies steady, continuous damage to all stressors in range (5 DPS base).
  * **Debuff:** Applies 50% slow to all stressors within the AoE radius (keeps them in range longer).
  * **Pulsing AoE:** Radius expands and contracts with breathing cycle â€” larger during exhale, smaller during inhale.
  * **Slow Behavior:** Slow persists while stressors are in range; clears when they leave the AoE.
  * **Affirm Interaction:** Slow strength is enhanced by Affirm amplification (stronger Affirm = stronger slow).
  * Serves as the game's stable "heartbeat" and primary defensive mechanism.
* **Visual Expression:**
  * A **soft circular bloom** of dye expands and contracts in rhythm with breathing, like a drop of color diffusing in water.
  * Colors shift gently between lavender, amber, and cerulean depending on Serenity, flowing like currents in liquid.
  * Dye feathers at the edges through fluid diffusion; concentration gradients create subtle halos as the liquid flows.
  * The entire scene subtly pulses â€” the liquid medium "breathes" with gentle currents.
  * Stressors within the bloom appear to move slower, their dye trails becoming more concentrated as they're caught in the calming current.
* **Liquid Techniques:** *Fluid diffusion, radial dispersion, dye concentration gradients, rhythmic flow patterns, current resistance.*
* **Self-Care Alignment:** Continuous breathwork creates a space of calm that slows and transforms stressors â€” the foundation of all self-care practices. The slow effect represents how focused breathing helps us respond thoughtfully rather than react impulsively.

## Design Notes

### Activation Patterns

* **Always Active:** Breathe, Align
* **Cooldown-Based Auto:** Recenter, Exhale, Mantra, Ground
* **Timed Amplifier:** Affirm
* **Reactive Defense:** Reflect, Release

### Mechanics Distribution

**By Category:**

* **Offensive:** Breathe, Exhale, Reflect, Mantra, Ground, Release (6 abilities)
* **Defensive:** Reflect (1 ability)
* **Buff:** Affirm, Release, Align (3 abilities)
* **Debuff:** Recenter, Exhale, Mantra, Ground, Release (5 abilities)
* **Non-offensive:** All 9 abilities (universal recovery/healing)

**By Ability Complexity:**

* **Simple (2-3 mechanics):** Breathe, Recenter, Mantra
* **Moderate (4 mechanics):** Exhale, Align
* **Complex (5 mechanics):** Affirm, Reflect, Ground, Release

### Scaling Philosophy

All abilities scale linearly with level upgrades, maintaining balance while providing meaningful progression. Each ability improves in multiple dimensions:

* Offensive abilities: Damage + utility (regen/debuff)
* Defensive abilities: Protection + recovery + counter-attack
* Buff abilities: Amplification + self-enhancement
* Debuff abilities: Control + damage reduction + vulnerability
* Non-offensive abilities: Recovery + capacity building

The liquid watermedia aesthetic ensures visual coherence regardless of power level.

### Visual Coherence

Every ability contributes to the unified liquid medium. No ability should feel "digital" or out of place â€” all effects should appear to flow through the same fluid medium, interacting through natural liquid dynamics.

### Self-Care Integration

Each ability represents a real-world self-care practice:

* **Breathe** = Continuous breathwork
* **Recenter** = Grounding techniques
* **Affirm** = Positive affirmations
* **Exhale** = Intentional breath release
* **Reflect** = Boundary setting
* **Mantra** = Mantra meditation
* **Ground** = Creating safe spaces
* **Release** = Letting go practices
* **Align** = Work-life balance

All mechanics support both active stress management and passive recovery, reflecting the dual nature of self-care: proactive protection and reactive healing.
